A Brief History of Football

American football evolved from soccer and rugby in the late 19th century. Walter Camp, a Yale player and coach, is widely regarded as the "Father of American Football" for establishing many of the game's fundamental rules — including the line of scrimmage and the down-and-distance system.

The National Football League (NFL) was founded in 1920 as the American Professional Football Association. Over the following century, the sport grew into the most-watched in the United States, with the Super Bowl becoming one of the most-viewed television events in the world each year.

Positions on the Field

Football has three distinct units, each with specialized roles:

  1. Offense
    • Quarterback (QB) — the field general who throws and manages the offense
    • Running Back (RB) — carries the ball on rushing plays
    • Wide Receiver (WR) — runs routes and catches passes downfield
    • Offensive Linemen (OL) — protect the QB and open running lanes
    • Tight End (TE) — hybrid blocker and receiver
  2. Defense
    • Defensive Linemen (DL) — disrupt the line of scrimmage
    • Linebacker (LB) — versatile players who cover and blitz
    • Cornerback (CB) — covers wide receivers
    • Safety (S) — the last line of defense in the secondary
  3. Special Teams
    • Kicker (K) — kicks field goals and extra points
    • Punter (P) — punts the ball on fourth down
    • Kick/Punt Returner — returns kicks for field position
